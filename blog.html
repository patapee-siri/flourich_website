Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis
Mental Health Chatbot

Chatbot-Delivered Interventions for Improving Mental Health Among Young People: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

Jiaying Li, Yan Li, Yule Hu, Dennis Chak Fai Ma, Xiaoxiao Mei, Engle Angela Chan, Janelle Yorke — The Hong Kong Polytechnic University

29 studies · 11 RCTs g=−0.28 effect size Age 10–24

Chatbot-delivered interventions significantly reduced psychological distress (Hedge's g=−0.28, 95% CI [−0.46, −0.10]) among young people. AI-based chatbots on instant messenger platforms showed strongest effects. Multimodal interaction improved outcomes compared to text-only. Provides evidence base for Flourich's design decisions.

Frontiers in Psychiatry · 2025

Rapid Systematic Review
College Students

Effectiveness of Artificial Intelligence Chatbots on Mental Health & Well-Being in College Students: A Rapid Systematic Review

Shahzadhi Nyakhar, Hongwu Wang — University of Florida

n=1,082 participants 8 chatbots 89% showed improvement

8 of 9 studies demonstrated statistically significant improvements in anxiety, depression, or well-being. Woebot reduced depression by 22% (PHQ-9: Δ=−3.16, p<0.05) in two weeks. CBT integration, daily interactions, and cultural personalization were key success factors — directly informing Flourich's design.

MentalChat16K: A Benchmark Dataset for Conversational Mental Health Assistance

Jia Xu, Tianyi Wei, Bojian Hou, et al. — University of Pennsylvania

16K QA pairs 7B LLM fine-tuning 33 topics

MentalChat16K combines synthetic counseling conversations and real interview transcripts covering depression, anxiety, and grief. Fine-tuned 7B LLMs significantly outperformed base models on 7 therapeutic metrics (Active Listening, Empathy, Safety). Foundation dataset for Flourich's conversational AI development.

Emotion Generation

Emotional Chatting Machine: Emotional Conversation Generation with Internal and External Memory

Hao Zhou, Minlie Huang, Tianyang Zhang, Xiaoyan Zhu — Tsinghua University; Bing Liu — University of Illinois

6 emotion categories Internal + External Memory 0.773 emotion accuracy

First work addressing emotion in large-scale conversation generation. ECM uses emotion category embedding, internal emotion memory, and external memory to generate emotionally consistent responses. Achieves 0.773 emotion accuracy vs 0.179 for basic seq2seq. Core technique behind Flourich's empathetic response system.

Technical Metrics Used to Evaluate Health Care Chatbots: Scoping Review

Alaa Abd-Alrazaq, Zeineb Safi, Mohannad Alajlani, Jim Warren, Mowafa Househ, Kerstin Denecke

65 studies 27 technical metrics Healthcare focus
